Section 2 ΓÇö Complete Pipeline Flow with Candidate Counts
This section replaces the previous pipeline table. The key architectural decision is the three-stage
funnel: title filter eliminates most irrelevant candidates first, bi-encoder narrows to a precise shortlist,
cross-encoder makes the final quality judgment. Each stage has a different speed/accuracy tradeoff.
2.1 The Three-Stage Funnel (Full 100K Dataset)
100,000 Candidates
(Full candidates.jsonl ΓÇö the competition dataset)
Γåô
STAGE 1 ΓÇö Title Pre-Filter
Lookup table. No embedding. Runs in ~2 seconds.
Γåô ~31,000 pass 69,000 eliminated (Business Analysts, HR, Accountants, Civil/Mech Eng, etc.)
STAGE 2 ΓÇö Bi-Encoder + Composite Scoring
Semantic similarity + 6 feature components. Vectorized numpy. ~15 seconds.
Γåô Top 300 Sorted by composite score. Only these 300 go to cross-encoder.
STAGE 3 ΓÇö Cross-Encoder Re-Ranking
Local ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2. Batch size 32. ~15ΓÇô30 seconds.
Γåô Top 100 Blended score: 40% algorithmic + 60% cross-encoder.
Final Submission ΓÇö Top 100 Ranked Candidates
CRITICAL RULE: Cross-encoder NEVER runs on all candidates. It only ever sees the shortlisted
top-300. This is intentional ΓÇö cross-encoder takes ~100ms per pair. On 100K that would take 2.8
hours. On 300 it takes 30 seconds. The bi-encoder's job is to narrow the field fast so the cross-
encoder can do deep, accurate judgment on a small set.

SKIP TITLE FILTER for small inputs: If input has fewer than 500 candidates, skip the title filter
entirely. Pass all candidates directly to bi-encoder scoring. This is because with 50 candidates
you want to rank all of them ΓÇö eliminating any would reduce the output below 100. The title filter
is a performance optimization for large datasets, not a quality gate.

WHY 300 IS THE RIGHT NUMBER: 300 is the sweet spot between coverage and speed. The
~745 genuine ML/AI candidates in the 100K dataset will almost all appear in the top-300 after
composite scoring. Going higher (500+) wastes CE budget on marginal candidates. Going lower
(100) risks missing good candidates the algorithm slightly underrated.

The One Thing to Get Right
MOST COMMON MISTAKE: Running cross-encoder BEFORE shortlisting. Always shortlist with
composite score first, THEN run cross-encoder on the shortlist. Never pass raw unsorted
candidates to the cross-encoder ΓÇö it will be slow and the quality gain from CE will be wasted on
irrelevant candidates.
